# Voice And Scenes

This reference distills the recurring language patterns from the NSTI materials into reusable guidance.

## Tone summary

The preferred tone is:

- warm but restrained
- observant rather than performative
- specific rather than slogan-heavy
- campus-facing rather than platform-facing
- literary in small doses, not line after line

The best lines usually feel like they came from someone who has walked through the place, not someone introducing it from a distance.

## Scene inventory

### Suiyuan

Use details such as:

- old buildings
- trees and shade
- corridors
- small slopes
- grass
- library
- the sense of quiet after walking inward a little

Good framing:

- "not loud at first glance"
- "a place that becomes clearer after you stay a little longer"
- "still in use, not sealed in the past"

### Xianlin and daily campus life

Use details such as:

- commuting between classes
- library study sessions
- shuttle buses
- club recruitment
- group work
- campus corners where people linger
- evening walks back to the dorm

Good framing:

- a lived rhythm
- practical routines
- crowd versus solitude
- pace, energy, and preference in daily choices

## Writing moves that usually work

### Move 1: Start with what is first noticed

Examples of first-notice elements:

- quiet
- tree shadow
- the pace of people passing through
- the feeling of a building and the space around it

### Move 2: Shift from place to habit

Do not stop at "the place is beautiful."

Move toward:

- how people slow down there
- how they make decisions there
- how they study, wait, meet, or pass through there

### Move 3: Keep a human scale

Focus on things a student can actually recognize:

- whether to stay a little longer
- whether to speak first in a group
- whether to go with a plan or improvise
- whether a person recovers in crowds or alone

## For question writing

One question works best when it has:

- one clear scene
- one concrete action
- four believable options
- one contrast underneath the surface

Avoid:

- lecture-like wording
- direct personality terminology
- options that sound morally better than others

## For project writing

When the user is the creator:

- say the user observed the problem
- say the user proposed the concept
- say the user chose the scene direction
- say AI or tools helped refine, structure, expand, implement, or generate

This keeps the narrative honest and avoids the common "AI did the thinking" tone.

## Red-flag wording

Try to reduce phrases that sound inflated or detached from student life, such as:

- empty declarations of growth
- abstract emotional universals
- forced inspirational endings
- over-polished branding lines with no real scene underneath

If a sentence can be replaced by a tree, a corridor, a bus stop, a group chat, or a library seat, it usually becomes better.
